Technical Experience

Crocs Of The Nial Javascript Game

Website: crocsofthenial.co.uk
Code: github.com

Niall has got relocated to GA in Egypt but he got lost on the river Nile on his way to work. Can you help him get accross the River to find GA's headquarters at "this.Pyramid". The game is simple get the boat accross the river intact, every time you have a bump a point is taken off your score. The most points wins. BEWARE theres a few surprises along the way.

This is a two player game powered by jQuery taking advantage of the animate and transform methods to make the crocodiles move and interact with the player. I was partically pleased with the moving river. The technologies I used were JavaScript, HTML, CSS, jQuery, jQuery - Animate and SoundManager.

Photo iQueue Rails App

Website: iPhoto.co.uk
Code: github.com

Photo iQueue was inspired from my love of photography. Its a photo storage app that allows you to store your favourite photos on a cloud server with details of where the photos were took including location maps and technical details.

This is a rails app working with a PostgreSQL database to store the users photos on AWS S3 server. I followed an Agile work flow to create this app with the following technologies, JavaScript, HTML, Ruby, Active Record, Rails, SCSS, jQuery and jQuery - Animate.
